Workout Splits

This section is to give you a few different workout routines that target every muscle group each week to ensure you are developing your body at an even rate and not letting any muscles not be worked.

Full Body Split: 3 Days a week

This workout routine is a routine where you would be in the gym 3 days out of the week. You would perform exercises on all muscle groups each training day. This routine will work if you only have a few days a week to get into the gym as your routine would be the following:

Monday: Whole Body
Tuesday: Off
Wednesday: Whole Body
Thursday: Off
Friday: Whole Body
Saturday: Off
Sunday: Off

Upper / Lower Split: 4 Days a week

This routine consist of one day training your upper body (chest, shoulders, triceps, biceps, back), and the following day training your lower body (quads, hamstrings, glutes, calves.) This routine is flexible if you have 4 days a week to make it to the gym as you will have 3 off days. Your schedule will look like the following:

Monday: Upper Body
Tuesday: Lower Body
Wednesday: Off
Thursday: Upper Body
Friday: Lower Body
Saturday: Off
Sunday: Off

The Bro Split: 5 Days a week

The Bro split or 5 days a week routine is one of the most popular workout splits due to its routine that is given to beginners when they first start to go to the gym to train. You will hit a different muscle group each day you train. With this split you can either train 5 days straight and take weekends off or put your first day off in between your training days. Your schedule will look like the following:

Monday: Chest
Tuesday: Shoulders
Wednesday: Back
Thursday: Arms
Friday: Legs
Saturday: Off
Sunday: Off

Push Pull Legs Split: 6 Days a week

PPL (Push Pull Legs) is a 6 day a week split with one rest day. Your push day will focus on your pushing movements, being chest, shoulders, and triceps. Your pull day will focus on your pulling movements, being back, biceps, and traps. Your leg day will focus on your lower body movements, being quads, hamstrings, glutes, and calves. This split primarily allows you to hit every muscle group twice a week for maximum growth. As you get deeper into fitness I believe this is a great routine to follow if you have 6 days to do strength training. Hitting your muscles twice a week will allow you to see more results in a shorter amount of time. With this split you will either have your rest day after your first leg session or after your second leg session. Your routine would look like the following:

Monday: Push
Tuesday: Pull
Wednesday: Legs
Thursday: Push
Friday: Pull
Saturday: Legs
Sunday: Off

Free For All Split: Any days a week (Not Recommended)

This free for all split is something I would not recommend. This means going into the gym and doing any type of exercise with a random amount of weight without any sense of direction. This may be okay if it is your first couple times in the gym just to get a feel for the atmosphere but I would not push that any further. Targetting the same muscle group consecutive days could lead to injury and muscle imbalances. This workout routine could look like the following:

Monday: Chest & Biceps
Tuesday: Biceps & Back
Wednesday: Chest
Thursday: Off
Friday: Chest & Shoulders
Saturday: Off
Sunday: Off
