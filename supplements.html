Supplements

What are Supplements?

When it comes to fitness, many people do not know what supplements are or which ones to take which is totally understandable because it can be overwhelming. The word "Supplement" is defined as, "somehing that completes or enhances something else when added to it." Supplements are not the magic ingredient to becoming stronger in the gym, or losing weight faster, they can help the process in a small way if used correctly. Using certain supplements can help you get the most out of trainng in the gym, such as higher energy levels, and a better level of focus. Taking un-researched supplements or supplements in high doses could be extremely bad for your health and body so be careful when doing so.

What supplements should I be taking?

Below are the supplements I take, and the top recommended to take with plenty of backed up research behind them and why people use them.



1. Creatine

Creatine is a molecule that's produced naturally in your body, and provides energy for your muscles and other tissues. By taking creatine you can increase muscle creatine content by up to 40% beyond its normal levels. This affects your muscle cells and exercise performance, promoting muscle gain. In fact, a large amount of research shows creatine improves muscle strength. Creatine can also increase water content in your muscle cells. This may cause your muscle cells to swell slightly and produce signals for muscle growth. Creatine being the most researched supplement on the market and being the most recommended and helpful is the reason why I have it being my number 1.

Overall, many researchers have studied creatine supplements and exercise, and one thing is clear — creatine can help increase muscle mass.

When using creatine, take 5g daily with water and that is all you need to do. Some labels recommend 3 servings of this for the first couple weeks called overloading, but you do not need to do this. This is on the label purely for marketing purposes and to make you go through your container faster to buy more. Also, when supplementing with creatine be sure to drink another 20 - 40 oz of water on top of your already daily intake.

2. Protein Supplements

Getting enough protein is critical for gaining muscle. Specifically, to gain muscle, you need to consume more protein than your body breaks down through natural processes. While it’s possible to get all the protein you need from protein-rich foods, some people struggle to do so. Now remember how I said getting 1g per lb of body weight is a good rule of thumb. If you are struggling to hit this number through eating your food, then a whey protein powder is the perfect solution for you.

On average a scoop of whey protein powder will give you 20 - 25g of protein, so if you are missing your protein numbers by anywheres from 25 - 50 grams then you would just need 1 - 2 scoops of protein to hit that number.

Protein powder is no magic ingredient to getting tons of muscle and becoming jacked, it is just there to help hit your protein number if you can not hit your number through food.

1 scoop of whey protein is just about the same thing as 4 ounces of skinless chicken breast.

3. Branch Chain Amino Acids

Branch Chain amino acids (BCAAs) consist of three individual amino acids: leucine, isoleucine and valine. They are found in most protein sources, particularly those of animal origin like meat, poultry, eggs, dairy and fish. BCAAs are critically important for muscle growth and make up about 14% of the amino acids in your muscles. A small amount of research has shown that BCAAs may improve muscle gain or reduce muscle loss, compared to a placebo.

The only reason I believe you should use BCAAs is when you are working on losing body fat, and you hit a point in your progress where you are starting to lose energy in the gym. Supplementing BCAAs in my own experience helps keep energy levels up, and muscle strength up while working out.

4. Pre-Work Out

Pre workout is in no shape or form something you should take if you do not like the added energy levels. Pre-workout supplements are taken to give you the edge to burn more fat, recover faster, or maximize your performance. The pre-workout you consume can also be tailored to improve a variety of factors such as endurance, strength, or muscle growth. Many pre-workout concoctions are offered as ‘stacks’ or combinations of different nutritional compounds to produce different effects in the user. Providing optimal nutrient intake prior to exercise will not only help you maximize your performance but also minimize muscle damage.

In my own experience I find that taking pre workout helps me get in the zone while working out at the gym and helps me concentrate on pushing heavier weights while also focusing on form control.

Preworkouts can also be damaging to your body if consuming too much at once, so be sure to do your research on everything you are taking prior to taking it.

5. Multivitamin

Multivitamins are supplements that contain many different vitamins and minerals, sometimes along with other ingredients. There is no real standard about what constitutes a multivitamin, and their nutrient composition varies by brand and product. They go by several different names, including multivitamins, multiminerals, multis, multiples or simply vitamins. They are available in many forms, such as tablets, capsules, chewable gummies, powders and liquids. Most multivitamins should be taken once or twice a day. Make sure to read the label and follow the recommended dosage instructions. At the end of the day, Multivitamins are supplements that contain many different vitamins and minerals. They are available in various forms.

You should really only be taking a multivitamin if you are not getting your main sources of vitamins through the foods you eat, but if you are following a realtively clean "diet" or eating habits then you will be getting all the vitamins you need, and there will be no need for a multi.
